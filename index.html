Trading Knowledge Base

What Is Forex?

Forex is the global currency exchange market where traders speculate on price movement between currencies. It is the most liquid financial market in the world.

Example: When you buy EUR/USD at 1.0800 and sell at 1.0850, you profit from a 50‑pip rise.

🌍 24h market – $7.5 trillion traded daily.

Pips

A pip is the smallest movement in most forex pairs. Traders use pips to measure profit, loss and spread.

1 pip ≈ $10.00

Standard account (1 lot = 100k units)

Risk Management

Serious traders focus more on protecting capital than chasing profits. Most professionals risk only 1-2% per trade.

Crypto Trading

Bitcoin and Ethereum are highly volatile digital assets. Volatility creates opportunity but also higher risk.

Example: BTC/USD moves $68,000 → $69,500. That's a $1,500 swing in minutes.

⚠️ Always use smaller position sizes due to extreme volatility.

Gold Trading

Gold reacts strongly to inflation, interest rates and global uncertainty. It is one of the most traded instruments worldwide.

Example: XAU/USD jumps from 2320 to 2345 on CPI news – a 250‑pip move.

📊 Often used as a safe‑haven during market turmoil.

Trading Psychology

Fear and greed destroy more traders than bad strategies. Psychology is what separates amateurs from professionals.

Scenario: After a losing trade, you immediately open a bigger position to "win it back".

That’s revenge trading — a classic emotional trap.

✅ Professional response: step away, review the trade, stick to the plan.
